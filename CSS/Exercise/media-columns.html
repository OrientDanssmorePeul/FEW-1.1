Article One

A beach is a landform consisting of loose particles like sand, pebbles, or shells, found along the edge of a body of water like an ocean or lake. Beaches are shaped by natural processes like wave action, tides, and sediment transport, and they can vary greatly in size and composition. They are popular for recreation and are important ecosystems for coastal plant and animal life.

Article Three

The sky is the expanse of space visible from the Earth's surface, encompassing the atmosphere, clouds, and celestial objects like the sun, moon, and stars. It's the area above us, extending upwards from the surface of a planet or moon. The sky can also be described as the visible portion of the celestial sphere, which is an imaginary sphere surrounding the Earth.
